🔍 Road Intel

Trap Types — what you're looking at

🔶
Overpass / Bridge
Officer at the apex — invisible until you pass under. Ka alert here means brake immediately. Antenna cluster visible above guardrail seconds before.
Look 0.3–0.5 mi ahead for the overpass silhouette and any parked shape at the top.
🟨
Median Crossover
Trooper parks in the median gap, often dark. LIDAR — no V1 warning until already targeted. Covers both directions simultaneously.
Every median turnout is a potential patrol spot. Note tire wear on the gravel.
🏍
Motorcycle / DragonEye LIDAR
Blends in traffic. Targets front AND rear. V1 may not alert until officer has already clocked you through a curve. No radar signature until impact.
I-35E south corridor: motorcycles run in the right lane before pulling to shoulder.
✈️
VASCAR Aircraft
Completely undetectable — no radar, no LIDAR. White painted triangles on pavement are timing gates. Single-engine Cessna or helo overhead.
Only counter: drive the limit. Active on I-94 St Paul and I-35W south. Look up.
🔀
On-Ramp / Merge Zone
Officer sits just past the acceleration lane. You're at 70+ before seeing them. Right shoulder immediately after the merge point.
Ease into flow speed gradually — don't punch it the moment you merge.
🚗
Shoulder Pullout
Standard setup. Dark shape between overhead signs, in sound wall shadow, or tucked behind an on-ramp nose. Look for single dim parking light or antenna outline.
MSP uses dark unmarked Chargers and Explorers — look for the roof antenna cluster and lightbar outline in rear window.

Behavioral cues from other drivers

🛑
Sudden brake lights with no cause
Cars ahead braking with no traffic, accident, or curve = someone spotted a trooper. Real-life Waze.
↩️
Left-lane drivers moving right
Multiple cars yielding right in quick succession means something is visible ahead that you haven't reached yet.
💡
Oncoming headlight flashes
The universal "cop ahead" signal. Still widely used. Flash back to pass it on.
🐢
Everyone bunching at limit
10 cars all doing exactly 65 in light traffic = something is pacing them. Don't pass the cluster.

Road geometry risk factors

⛰️
Downhill grades
Cars naturally gain speed on descents. Troopers position at the bottom where speeds peak. Common on I-35E river bluffs.
🔄
After curves
Drivers naturally accelerate out of bends. Officer positions just past the curve exit where the straight begins.
📉
Speed limit drops
Sudden drops (65→55→45) with minimal advance signage. I-94 entering St Paul is the metro's prime example — most drivers are still doing 65 in the 45 zone.
▶️
"Resume Speed" zones
Everyone accelerates simultaneously after construction ends. Trooper positioned 0.2–0.5 mi past the sign.

Time & context patterns

📅
End of month (last 5 days)
Citation rates spike as troopers close out monthly stats. Statistically the most dangerous week to speed on any corridor.
🔃
Shift change windows
6–8 AM · 2–4 PM · 10 PM–midnight = 15–20 min patrol gaps as troopers return to station. Coverage at its lowest in these windows.
🌧️
Light rain
Counterintuitively increases enforcement — troopers are still out, speeds are lower, so 70 in a 55 is more obvious. Heavy rain/snow reduces it.
🎉
Post-holiday surge end
First Tuesday after a holiday weekend = enforcement normalizes. The surge is over but vigilance drops — troopers still active at near-surge levels.
