V1 + Waze Optimal Setup — MN
📡
V1 settings for MN metro: Enable Ka full, K reduced (high false-positives from auto doors). Disable X-band in city. Use Logic Mode if V1 Gen2.
📱
Waze as laser trap spotter: Laser is always from a stationary position — Waze users mark these spots. Set Waze to "Alerts Only" mode and route your drive even if you know the route. Advance alert range: ¼–½ mile (but confirms stationary threat).
🔗
V1 + Waze integration: Use YaV1 or V1Driver Bluetooth app to run V1 signal + Waze audio on same device. Both apps filter K-band false alerts and annotate signal direction from V1's arrow display.
🏍️
Motorcycle cop tell: DragonEye LIDAR is used by MN motorcycle units. They typically sit on entrance ramps or behind barriers. V1 may not alert. Waze + traffic ahead as proxy — slow cars ahead near a ramp = possible motorcycle unit.
✈️
Aircraft stretches: I-94 westbound past Maple Grove, I-35 south of Lakeville. "SPEED ENFORCED BY AIRCRAFT" signs are legal warning. No detector helps. Fly at 5 over max with flow of traffic.
🚗
Best passive protection: Stay behind a buffer of 4+ cars. Let faster drivers act as scouts. In the Twin Cities, the flow of traffic on interstates typically runs 72–77 mph — being the fastest car by >5 mph is the primary selection criteria for enforcement.
Flock Safety Cameras — What They Are (and Are Not)
What Flock cameras DO:
🔤
Read and log your license plate, timestamp, and location every time you pass. Data stored in searchable database accessible by all MN law enforcement agencies.
🚨
Automatically alert if your plate matches a stolen vehicle list, Amber Alert, or law enforcement lookout.
🔍
Log vehicle make, model, color, and distinguishing features (roof racks, stickers, etc.) using AI.
🕸️
300+ units in Twin Cities metro. Concentrated in: Wayzata, Eden Prairie, Plymouth, Minnetonka, Brooklyn Park, Edina, and near U of MN campus.
What Flock cameras DO NOT do:
Flock cameras are NOT speed enforcement. They do not measure speed, cannot issue citations, and do not trigger police stops for speeding. No V1 or Waze countermeasure needed.
❌
Do not record video or audio. Do not use facial recognition. Do not enforce parking or traffic signals.
⚠️
Privacy note: MN legislature actively debating data retention limits (2026). Some suburbs (Brooklyn Park, Shorewood) have shut cameras off. Federal immigration use has prompted additional scrutiny.

The Stop — Optimal Script by Phase
⏹ Phase 1: Pulling Over
Action — not words:
Signal immediately, pull to the right shoulder smoothly and completely. Hazard lights on. Engine off or in park. Hands visible on steering wheel before officer approaches. Do NOT reach for anything until asked.
🪟 Phase 2: First Contact
Say exactly:
"Good [morning/afternoon/evening], officer." Then stop. Let them lead. Hand over license, registration, and proof of insurance when asked — not before. Keep hands visible during every reach.
Do NOT say "I know why you pulled me over" or anything that admits speed. Silence is neutral. Admission is permanent.
❓ Phase 3: "Do you know how fast you were going?"
Best response — non-committal and polite:
"I'm sorry, I'm not certain. What did you have me at?"
💡
This is not lying — you genuinely may not know their exact reading. It keeps the record clean, invites them to state the measurement (which you'll need for court), and projects calm confidence.
🚫 Phase 4: Never say these
❌ "I was just keeping up with traffic" — admission of speeding
❌ "I was only going [X]" — you've now confirmed a speed
❌ "Your radar must be wrong" — immediately antagonistic
❌ "Can't you just give me a warning?" — rarely works, sounds entitled
❌ "I'm late for [anything]" — not a legal defense, just context for a story
Maximizing Outcome Probability at the Roadside
😌
Project calm confidence. Officers write fewer tickets to people who are composed, cooperative, and professional. Nervousness can read as evasiveness; aggression kills any chance of a warning.
📝
Note everything immediately after. Write down: time, location, officer's name and badge number, exact speed stated, direction of travel, weather, traffic conditions, what you were doing. This is your discovery foundation.
📷
Dashcam silently helps. Footage can confirm road conditions, traffic flow (pacing defense), and your behavior at the stop. Don't mention it unless it helps you.
🤐
Don't argue the law at the roadside. The officer cannot dismiss your ticket there. Any legal argument is wasted — and may be used against you. Save it for the hearing.
🙏
The "please" moment: After accepting the citation politely, you can say: "Officer, I have a clean driving record and this was a lapse in judgment. Is there any chance this could be reduced?" — No worse than silence. Works occasionally, especially end-of-shift or not-end-of-month.
If You Think the Stop Was Improper
⚖️
Do NOT argue it at the scene. If the stop was unlawful (no probable cause), that's a suppression argument for court. Arguing it roadside accomplishes nothing except escalation.
🧾
Accept the citation, note your objection mentally, then consult an attorney. Fourth Amendment suppression (if the stop was pretextual or without cause) is a powerful defense that wins dismissals — but only in court.
When You're in a Construction or School Zone
🚧
Doubled fines are mandatory — but polite behavior at the stop doesn't change the fine amount. Focus energy on contesting in court (calibration records, zone marking compliance) rather than roadside negotiation.
📣
For school zone automated camera citations: officer interaction is irrelevant — all contestation is by mail or in-person hearing. Note the exact date/time and whether school was actually in session.
